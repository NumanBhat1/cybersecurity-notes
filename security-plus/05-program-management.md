# Domain 5: Security Program Management & Oversight (20%)

Governance, risk, and compliance - the "management" side.

## Risk management

- **Risk = likelihood x impact.**
- **Risk responses:** accept, avoid, transfer (insurance), mitigate.
- **Risk appetite / tolerance** - how much risk an org accepts.
- **Risk register** - documented list of risks + owners + status.
- **Inherent vs residual risk** - before vs after controls.

### Quantitative risk (formulas)

- **SLE (Single Loss Expectancy) = Asset Value x Exposure Factor**
- **ALE (Annual Loss Expectancy) = SLE x ARO**
- **ARO** = Annual Rate of Occurrence (how many times/year).

### Qualitative risk

Uses ratings (low/medium/high) instead of dollar values.

## Business continuity & disaster recovery

- **BIA (Business Impact Analysis)** - identify critical functions + impact of loss.
- **BCP** - keep the business running during disruption.
- **DRP** - restore IT after a disaster.
- **RTO / RPO** - max downtime / max data loss tolerated.
- **MTTR / MTBF** - mean time to repair / between failures.

## Governance

- **Policies** - high-level rules (e.g. AUP - Acceptable Use Policy).
- **Standards** - specific mandatory requirements.
- **Procedures** - step-by-step instructions.
- **Guidelines** - recommendations (not mandatory).
- **Governance structures** - boards, committees, ownership.

## Third-party / vendor risk

- **Due diligence** before engaging a vendor.
- **SLA** - service level agreement (uptime/performance).
- **MOU / MOA** - memorandum of understanding/agreement.
- **BPA** - business partner agreement.
- **NDA** - non-disclosure agreement.
- **Supply chain risk** - vendor compromise affects you.

## Compliance & frameworks

- **PCI DSS** - payment card data.
- **HIPAA** - US healthcare data.
- **GDPR** - EU data privacy.
- **SOC 2** - service org controls (trust criteria).
- **ISO 27001** - infosec management standard.
- **NIST CSF / 800-53** - US frameworks.

## Security awareness

- Training, phishing simulations, recognizing social engineering, reporting,
  acceptable use. People are often the weakest link.
